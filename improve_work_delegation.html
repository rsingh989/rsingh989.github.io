METHODS FOR FOUNDERS TO IMPROVE WORK DELEGATION

When you’re on the journey to starting your business, or taking it to new heights, you feel very excited at the glamorous opportunity of doing everything by yourself. But then, so can skydiving. There is a reason though that people don’t leap out of an airborne vessel without a parachute — the same one why you should not lead your company without accepting the value of how important it is to learn to delegate work.

No doubt, it will be one of the most challenging skills to master. Maybe you have always been a person who prefers to have the hands-on experience with things, and you just cannot bear the idea of losing a grip on its creation process. Regardless of what it is though, the only thing you’re doing by trying to handle everything yourself is hampering the growth rate of your company.

Even if there is the possibility that you can do a task better than someone else, your time can be put to enter use elsewhere. This is where the process of strategising your delegation comes into play. It is not ideal for a leader just to give out a few easy assignments and call it a day. And you especially do not want to take the knee-jerk approach and distribute all of your tasks to your subordinates who already have a lot of work on their plate.

To become a master of delegation, you need to be mentally prepared to say goodbye to some of the tasks you believe should be yours during the day. We have three strategies for you that will help you maintain just enough control over your processes without evolving into a micromanager.

1. Embed a belief that you shouldn’t do it all

You know that you are not a superhuman, and that is one tough pill to swallow, even though you might have always adopted a lone wolf style of working. Even though the entrepreneurial side of your brain will push back against this notion of you being a super-productive army of one, you may end up burning out if you push yourself way too hard, which 52% of working professionals have already experienced, according to an Indeed survey.

You can achieve this by dividing up your activities throughout the day into two separate stacks. The first stack should consist of those activities which you consider would be best done by either you or some other executive, which the second stack should consist of those activities which can be completed by anyone else without any hassle. It is also important to remember that the activities involving a lot of tasks can be further split and partially delegated, to improve productivity and efficiency.

Now that you have separated all your activities, you can take of all of your activities from the second stack and delegate them to employees in your organisation, or even independent contractors such as consultants or freelancers. This frees up time and energy for you to focus on all the tasks that really matter.

2. Even startups have to have standard operating procedures

If you still feel a little stressed out about handing over responsibilities, particularly to some team members who might be newly recruited and are yet to be tested, then maybe it is time to improve your processes and introduce standardisation across the board for everything that your business does. These can be for activities that are repeated on a regular-basis, or even for one-off procedures.

If you are looking for inspiration for setting up your standard operating procedures, just know that you are not out there to reinvent the wheel on how companies are or should be run. There are several sources out there, such as Betterteam and SHRM with starter templates for defining various employee processes and policies to begin with and customise to your own fancies, so you, your business and your employees all perform at the highest level.

3. Monitor the task delegation with project management tools

As important as it is to delegate a task, this does not mean that you would not want to keep a tab on the progress that is being made on it. This would be as worse as assigning a task to someone and looking over their shoulder all the time, because not only would these waste any returns you would have received, but also drop your employee’s confidence and make them feel less trusted and valued, which has been one of the biggest reasons for employees to quit their jobs.

There are plenty of technology stacks out there that help you monitor the process of the tasks that have been assigned to people at different levels of the organisation, individual and collective work alike. All that you have to do is set up a standardised software that is used by every employee in your company for the purpose of creating and delegating tasks as well as establish fast and efficient lines of communication and reporting.

Even though you would not be the most ideal candidate to win the award for being the best delegator, and that will be fine. But you can certainly grow your skills in the delegation department to benefit and protect you, grow your trust with your employees and help them grow professionally as well.
